Many English learners understand tenses but still sound unnatural because they rarely use **Introductory "There"** and **Introductory (Dummy) "It."**

Think of them as **sentence starters**.

They don't have much meaning themselves—they help introduce information naturally.

---

# The Big Idea

There are two kinds of "there" and two kinds of "it."

### 1. Real "There" (Location)

> I left my phone **there**.

বাংলা:

আমি আমার ফোনটা **ওখানে** রেখে এসেছি।

Here, **there** means **ওখানে**.

---

### 2. Introductory "There"

> There is a problem.

Here, "there" **doesn't mean "ওখানে."**

It simply introduces something.

---

Similarly,

### Real "It"

> I bought a phone. **It** is expensive.

"It" refers to the phone.

---

### Introductory "It"

> It is raining.

What does "it" refer to?

Nothing.

It's just the natural way English talks about weather.

---

# PART 1: Introductory THERE

Imagine you walk into a room.

Instead of saying

> A cat is under the table.

Native speakers often say

> **There is** a cat under the table.

Why?

Because you're introducing new information.

Think:

"There exists..."

---

Example

> There is a new restaurant near my house.

বাংলা:

আমার বাড়ির কাছে একটি নতুন রেস্টুরেন্ট আছে।

You're introducing the restaurant.

---

# When Do Native Speakers Use "There"?

### 1. To say something exists

> There is a mistake.

একটি ভুল আছে।

---

> There are two buses outside.

বাইরে দুটি বাস আছে।

---

### 2. To introduce something new

Instead of

> A man was waiting outside.

Natural:

> There was a man waiting outside.

This sounds much more like storytelling.

---

### 3. To describe places

> There is a bank on this street.

> There are many people here.

> There was a loud noise.

---

# There Is vs It Is

This confuses nearly everyone.

## There is...

means

**Something exists.**

> There is a dog outside.

একটি কুকুর বাইরে আছে।

---

## It is...

describes something already known.

> The dog is outside.

Or

> It is outside.

Now everyone knows which dog.

---

Think like this:

First mention

↓

There is a dog.

Second mention

↓

The dog is...

↓

It is...

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ It has a book on the table.

Correct:

> There is a book on the table.

---

❌ There is my brother. (possible in some contexts but uncommon)

Usually:

> My brother is here.

---

# PART 2: Introductory IT

Unlike "there,"

"It" often talks about:

* weather
* time
* distance
* conditions
* opinions

---

# Weather

English always uses "it."

> It is raining.

বৃষ্টি হচ্ছে।

---

> It is snowing.

তুষারপাত হচ্ছে।

---

> It was sunny yesterday.

গতকাল রোদ ছিল।

---

Notice:

There is raining ❌

Never.

---

# Time

> What time is it?

কয়টা বাজে?

---

> It is five o'clock.

পাঁচটা বাজে।

---

# Distance

> It is five kilometers from here.

এখান থেকে পাঁচ কিলোমিটার।

---

# Temperature

> It is very hot today.

আজ খুব গরম।

---

> It is cold outside.

---

# Days

> It is Monday.

আজ সোমবার।

---

# Dates

> It is July 8.

আজ ৮ জুলাই।

---

# Opinions

Native speakers LOVE this pattern.

Instead of

> Learning English is difficult.

They often say

> It is difficult to learn English.

Both are correct.

The second sounds more natural in conversation.

---

More examples

> It is important to sleep well.

ভালো ঘুমানো গুরুত্বপূর্ণ।

---

> It is easy to understand.

---

> It is impossible to know everything.

---

# Why Use Introductory "It"?

Compare.

Learning English is important.

Good.

---

It is important to learn English.

Much smoother.

Native speakers often put the long subject at the end.

This is called **end-weight**.

English prefers not to start with long subjects.

---

# Another Common Pattern

It seems...

> It seems that he's busy.

মনে হচ্ছে সে ব্যস্ত।

---

It looks like...

> It looks like it's going to rain.

মনে হচ্ছে বৃষ্টি হবে।

---

It appears...

> It appears that we were wrong.

---

It turns out...

> It turns out I was right.

শেষ পর্যন্ত দেখা গেল আমি ঠিক ছিলাম।

Very common.

---

# It Takes...

Native speakers use this constantly.

> It takes two hours to reach Dhaka.

ঢাকায় যেতে দুই ঘণ্টা লাগে।

---

> It took me ten minutes to finish.

আমার দশ মিনিট লেগেছিল।

---

# It Depends

One of the most common English sentences.

> It depends.

নির্ভর করছে।

---

> It depends on the weather.

---

# It Doesn't Matter

> It doesn't matter.

কোনো সমস্যা নেই।

---

# It Sounds...

> It sounds good.

---

> It sounds interesting.

---

# It Feels...

> It feels strange.

---

# It Smells...

> It smells nice.

---

# It Looks...

> It looks beautiful.

---

# It Means...

> What does it mean?

এর অর্থ কী?

---

# There Used With Different Tenses

Present

> There is a problem.

---

Past

> There was a problem.

---

Future

> There will be a meeting tomorrow.

---

Present Perfect

> There has been an accident.

---

Past Perfect

> There had been several complaints.

---

# Another Confusing Pattern

There seems to be...

Very common.

> There seems to be a mistake.

মনে হচ্ছে একটি ভুল আছে।

---

There appears to be...

More formal.

---

There used to be...

> There used to be a cinema here.

এখানে আগে একটি সিনেমা হল ছিল।

---

# Common Fixed Expressions

### There you go!

Used when handing something over or when someone finally understands.

> Here are your keys.

> There you go!

---

### There you are!

Often used when you find someone or hand them something.

---

### There we are.

Means

Finished.

---

### There you have it.

Means

That's the explanation.

---
