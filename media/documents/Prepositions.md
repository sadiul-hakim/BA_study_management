The bad news is: there are many prepositions.

The good news is: about **80% of everyday English uses a relatively small group of them.**

---

# What Is a Preposition?

A preposition shows a relationship between words.

Usually:

* Place
* Time
* Direction
* Cause
* Method

Examples:

* The book is **on** the table.
* I arrived **at** 8 PM.
* She walked **into** the room.

---

# PART 1: PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE

## 1. IN

Think:

**Inside something**

### English

The cat is in the box.

### Bangla

বিড়ালটি বাক্সের ভিতরে আছে।

---

### English

I live in Bangladesh.

### Bangla

আমি বাংলাদেশে থাকি।

---

Used for:

* Countries
* Cities
* Rooms
* Buildings
* Boxes
* Containers

Examples:

* in Dhaka
* in Bangladesh
* in my room
* in the car

---

## 2. ON

Think:

**Touching a surface**

### English

The book is on the table.

### Bangla

বইটি টেবিলের উপর আছে।

---

### English

There is a picture on the wall.

### Bangla

দেয়ালে একটি ছবি আছে।

---

Examples:

* on the floor
* on the desk
* on the wall
* on the roof

---

## 3. AT

Think:

**A point or specific location**

### English

I am at home.

### Bangla

আমি বাড়িতে আছি।

---

### English

She is at the bus stop.

### Bangla

সে বাসস্ট্যান্ডে আছে।

---

Examples:

* at school
* at work
* at home
* at the station

---

# IN vs ON vs AT

Imagine:

```
Bangladesh
   ↓
Dhaka
   ↓
House
```

We say:

* I live **in Bangladesh**.
* I live **in Dhaka**.
* I am **at my house**.

# PART 2: PREPOSITIONS OF TIME

## 1. IN

Used for:

* Months
* Years
* Centuries

### English

I was born in 1998.

### Bangla

আমি ১৯৯৮ সালে জন্মেছি।

---

### English

Winter comes in December.

### Bangla

শীত ডিসেম্বর মাসে আসে।

---

Examples:

* in 2025
* in January
* in winter

---

## 2. ON

Used for:

* Days
* Dates

### English

I will meet you on Sunday.

### Bangla

আমি রবিবার তোমার সাথে দেখা করব।

---

### English

My birthday is on 15 March.

### Bangla

আমার জন্মদিন ১৫ মার্চ।

---

Examples:

* on Monday
* on Friday
* on 10 July

---

## 3. AT

Used for:

* Specific times

### English

The class starts at 8 AM.

### Bangla

ক্লাস সকাল ৮টায় শুরু হয়।

---

Examples:

* at noon
* at midnight
* at 5 PM

---

# Memory Trick

### Time

* IN → month/year
* ON → day/date
* AT → exact time

Examples:

* in 2025
* on Monday
* at 8 PM

# PART 3: DIRECTION

## TO

### English

I am going to school.

### Bangla

আমি স্কুলে যাচ্ছি।

---

### English

Send it to me.

### Bangla

এটা আমাকে পাঠাও।

---

## INTO

Movement from outside to inside.

### English

The cat jumped into the box.

### Bangla

বিড়ালটি বাক্সের ভিতরে লাফ দিল।

---

## OUT OF

Movement from inside to outside.

### English

She came out of the room.

### Bangla

সে ঘর থেকে বের হলো।

---

# PART 4: FOR and SINCE

These are extremely important.

---

## FOR

Used for a duration.

### English

I have lived here for three years.

### Bangla

আমি এখানে তিন বছর ধরে বাস করছি।

---

Duration:

* for two days
* for three months
* for ten years

---

## SINCE

Used for a starting point.

### English

I have lived here since 2020.

### Bangla

আমি ২০২০ সাল থেকে এখানে বাস করছি।

---

Examples:

* since Monday
* since January
* since 2020

---

# FOR vs SINCE

### For = How long?

For three years.

তিন বছর ধরে।

---

### Since = From when?

Since 2020.

২০২০ সাল থেকে।

---

# PART 5: BY vs UNTIL

---

## BY

Before a deadline.

### English

Finish the work by Friday.

### Bangla

শুক্রবারের মধ্যে কাজ শেষ করো।

---

Meaning:

Any time before Friday.

---

## UNTIL

Continue up to a time.

### English

I worked until midnight.

### Bangla

আমি মধ্যরাত পর্যন্ত কাজ করেছি।

---

# PART 6: WITH vs BY

---

## WITH

Tool or instrument.

### English

I cut the cake with a knife.

### Bangla

আমি ছুরি দিয়ে কেক কাটলাম।

---

## BY

Person or method.

### English

The book was written by Shakespeare.

### Bangla

বইটি শেক্সপিয়ার লিখেছিলেন।

---

আকাশের দিকে তাকাও।

---

## Wait for

# The 15 Most Important Prepositions

If you master these, you'll cover most daily English:

| Preposition | Main Meaning       |
| ----------- | ------------------ |
| in          | inside, month/year |
| on          | surface, day/date  |
| at          | point, exact time  |
| to          | direction          |
| into        | move inside        |
| out of      | move outside       |
| for         | duration           |
| since       | starting point     |
| by          | deadline, agent    |
| until       | up to a time       |
| with        | tool               |
| from        | source             |
| about       | topic              |
| of          | belonging          |
| over        | above              |

# Final Advice

Unlike tenses, **prepositions are not learned mainly through rules.**

You learn them through **exposure and repetition**:

* read stories,
* read articles,
* watch videos,
* notice phrases.

For example, don't memorize only **"interested"**. Memorize:

> interested in

Don't memorize only **"depend"**. Memorize:

> depend on

Don't memorize only **"good"**. Memorize:

> good at

Treat them as a single unit. This is how native speakers learn them, and it's the fastest way to sound natural.
