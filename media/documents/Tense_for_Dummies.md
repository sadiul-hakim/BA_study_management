
Let's use **one story** and include **all 12 English tenses**.

---

# A Story Using All 12 Tenses

## 1. Present Indefinite

**English:** I study English every day.

**Bangla:** আমি প্রতিদিন ইংরেজি পড়ি।

**Why?**
A habitual action or routine.

**Formula:** Subject + Verb₁

---

## 2. Present Continuous

**English:** I am studying English right now.

**Bangla:** আমি এখন ইংরেজি পড়ছি।

**Why?**
The action is happening at this moment.

**Formula:** am/is/are + Verb-ing

---

## 3. Present Perfect

**English:** I have studied three chapters today.

**Bangla:** আমি আজ তিনটি অধ্যায় পড়েছি।

**Why?**
The action is finished, but its result is important now.

The chapters are already completed.

**Formula:** have/has + Verb₃

---

## 4. Present Perfect Continuous

**English:** I have been studying English for three years.

**Bangla:** আমি তিন বছর ধরে ইংরেজি পড়ছি।

**Why?**
The action started in the past and is still continuing.

**Keywords:** for, since

**Formula:** have/has been + Verb-ing

---

# Past Tenses

## 5. Past Indefinite

**English:** I studied English yesterday.

**Bangla:** আমি গতকাল ইংরেজি পড়েছিলাম।

**Why?**
A completed action in the past.

**Formula:** Verb₂

---

## 6. Past Continuous

**English:** I was studying English when my friend called me.

**Bangla:** আমার বন্ধু ফোন করার সময় আমি ইংরেজি পড়ছিলাম।

**Why?**
An action was in progress at a specific time in the past.

**Formula:** was/were + Verb-ing

---

## 7. Past Perfect

**English:** I had studied English before the exam started.

**Bangla:** পরীক্ষা শুরু হওয়ার আগে আমি ইংরেজি পড়ে নিয়েছিলাম।

**Why?**
Two past actions.

The studying happened first.
The exam started later.

**Earlier Past → Past Perfect**

```
Study ---> Exam
(had studied)
```

**Formula:** had + Verb₃

---

## 8. Past Perfect Continuous

**English:** I had been studying English for two hours before dinner.

**Bangla:** রাতের খাবারের আগে আমি দুই ঘণ্টা ধরে ইংরেজি পড়ছিলাম।

**Why?**
An action continued for some time before another past event.

```
Studying (2 hours) ---> Dinner
```

**Formula:** had been + Verb-ing

---

# Future Tenses

## 9. Future Indefinite

**English:** I will study English tomorrow.

**Bangla:** আমি আগামীকাল ইংরেজি পড়ব।

**Why?**
A future action.

**Formula:** will + Verb₁

---

## 10. Future Continuous

**English:** I will be studying English at 8 PM tomorrow.

**Bangla:** আগামীকাল রাত ৮টায় আমি ইংরেজি পড়তে থাকব।

**Why?**
An action will be in progress at a future time.

```
Tomorrow 8 PM
      ↓
Studying
```

**Formula:** will be + Verb-ing

---

## 11. Future Perfect

**English:** I will have studied ten chapters by next week.

**Bangla:** আগামী সপ্তাহের মধ্যে আমি দশটি অধ্যায় পড়ে ফেলব।

**Why?**
The action will be completed before a future point.

```
Now ---> Study ---> Next Week
              (completed)
```

**Formula:** will have + Verb₃

---

## 12. Future Perfect Continuous

**English:** By next month, I will have been studying English for four years.

**Bangla:** আগামী মাসে আমার ইংরেজি শেখা চার বছর ধরে চলতে থাকবে।

**Why?**
An action starts before a future point and continues up to that future point.

```
Past ----------------> Next Month
      studying
```

**Formula:** will have been + Verb-ing

---

# The Four Tenses That Usually Confuse Learners

### Present Perfect vs Past Indefinite

✅ I have eaten lunch.
(আমি দুপুরের খাবার খেয়েছি।)

Focus: Result now.

✅ I ate lunch at 1 PM.
(আমি দুপুর ১টায় খাবার খেয়েছিলাম।)

Focus: Specific past time.

---

### Present Continuous vs Present Perfect Continuous

✅ I am reading a book.

আমি এখন একটি বই পড়ছি।

(At this moment.)

✅ I have been reading this book for two weeks.

আমি দুই সপ্তাহ ধরে এই বই পড়ছি।

(Started earlier and still continuing.)

---

### Past Perfect

✅ I had finished my work before my boss arrived.

আমি বস আসার আগে কাজ শেষ করেছিলাম।

Rule:

**Earlier past action → Past Perfect**

**Later past action → Past Indefinite**

---

### Future Perfect

✅ I will have finished the project by Friday.

আমি শুক্রবারের মধ্যে প্রকল্প শেষ করে ফেলব।

Think:

"Before a future deadline."

# A Quick Memory Trick

| Tense Type         | Main Idea          |
| ------------------ | ------------------ |
| Indefinite         | Simple fact/action |
| Continuous         | Action in progress |
| Perfect            | Completed action   |
| Perfect Continuous | Duration of action |

Then apply them to Present, Past, and Future:

| Time    | Indefinite   | Continuous         | Perfect             | Perfect Continuous        |
| ------- | ------------ | ------------------ | ------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Present | I study      | I am studying      | I have studied      | I have been studying      |
| Past    | I studied    | I was studying     | I had studied       | I had been studying       |
| Future  | I will study | I will be studying | I will have studied | I will have been studying |

A useful observation: in everyday conversation, native speakers use **Present Indefinite, Present Continuous, Past Indefinite, and Future Indefinite** most of the time. **Perfect** and **Perfect Continuous** tenses are used less often, which is why they may feel unfamiliar even if you understand them.
