A **determiner** is a word that comes **before a noun** and gives information about it, such as **which one, whose, how many, or how much**.

Think of determiners as "noun introducers."

### Examples

* **a** book
* **an** apple
* **the** car
* **my** phone
* **this** house
* **some** water
* **three** students
* **each** person

In all these examples, the determiner comes before the noun.

---

## Main Types of Determiners

### 1. Articles

* **a**, **an**, **the**

Examples:

* I bought **a** pen.
* She ate **an** orange.
* **The** sun is shining.

---

### 2. Demonstratives

* **this**, **that**, **these**, **those**

Examples:

* **This** book is interesting.
* **Those** students are absent.

---

### 3. Possessives

* **my**, **your**, **his**, **her**, **its**, **our**, **their**

Examples:

* **My** brother is a teacher.
* **Their** house is large.

---

### 4. Quantifiers

Show quantity.

* **some**, **any**, **much**, **many**, **little**, **few**, **all**, **enough**

Examples:

* I have **some** money.
* There are **many** people here.

---

### 5. Numbers

* **one**, **two**, **three**, etc.

Examples:

* **Two** books are missing.
* **Ten** students passed.

---

### 6. Distributives

Refer to members of a group individually.

* **each**, **every**, **either**, **neither**

Examples:

* **Each** student received a certificate.
* **Every** child needs care.

---

### 7. Interrogative Determiners

Used in questions.

* **which**, **what**, **whose**

Examples:

* **Which** book do you want?
* **Whose** bag is this?

---

## Determiner vs Pronoun

A word like **this** can be either a determiner or a pronoun.

**Determiner:**

* **This book** is mine.

  * "This" modifies the noun "book."

**Pronoun:**

* **This** is mine.

  * "This" replaces the noun.

Similarly:

* **My phone** is new. → "my" is a determiner.
* This phone is **mine**. → "mine" is a pronoun.

---

### Quick Rule

If the word comes **before a noun**, it is usually a **determiner**.

* **the car**
* **my house**
* **those students**
* **three books**

If it **stands alone** and replaces a noun, it is usually a **pronoun**.

* **This** is expensive.
* **Those** are mine.
* The red car is **hers**.

So, in simple terms:

> **Determiners come before nouns and help identify or limit them.**
> **Pronouns replace nouns.**
